# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Carecierge serves people who want practical help managing, automating, and strengthening their personal relationships. They are not looking for another generic productivity dashboard; they are looking for a trusted advisor that helps them notice what matters, follow through at the right time, and use additional app support without losing the warmth or familiarity of human care.

Unauthenticated marketing pages will exist later, but the default product context is authenticated relationship-management work.

## Product Purpose

Carecierge helps users maintain better relationships by combining organization, automation, reminders, and supportive guidance around the people in their lives. Success means users feel more prepared, attentive, and confident in how they care for their relationships, while still feeling that the product respects the intimacy and trust of that domain.

## Brand Personality

Warm, caring, trustworthy. The interface should feel friendly, familiar, and advisor-like: calm enough for sensitive personal context, clear enough to support action, and human enough that relationship work does not feel mechanized.

## Anti-references

Carecierge should not feel like a cold enterprise CRM, a clinical healthcare portal, a surveillance-oriented AI assistant, a generic chatbot, or a luxury concierge site. It should avoid sterile productivity patterns, performative automation, status-heavy dashboards, and visual cues that make relationships feel like leads, tickets, patients, or transactions.

## Design Principles

1. Trust before automation: automation should feel explainable, reversible, and in service of the user's intent.
2. Care is concrete: guide users toward specific next steps instead of vague emotional copy or decorative warmth.
3. Familiar beats flashy: use recognizable product patterns so users can focus on relationship work, not interface interpretation.
4. Private by default: treat relationship data as sensitive, with clear boundaries around visibility, sharing, and system action.
5. Support the moment: prioritize timely prompts, gentle recovery paths, and low-friction follow-through over broad dashboard spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use industry-standard accessibility practices as the baseline: WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible workflows, visible focus states, semantic markup, reduced-motion support, and color-blind-safe states. Interaction patterns should remain understandable without relying on color, animation, or pointer-only controls.
